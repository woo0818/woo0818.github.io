SceneMI: Scene-aware Motion In-betweening

Anonymous CVPR submission

Abstract

Existing motion in-betweening research focuses on isolated character movements, often overlooking the impact of the surrounding environment. However, in real-world scenarios, movements are essentially shaped by the context—such as a character adjusting their position and posture to avoid obstacles. In this work, we introduce Scene-aware Motion In-betweening (SceneMI), a framework that generates transitions between keyframes that seamlessly adapt to the environment.

To effectively encode scene context, we employ two kinds of scene descriptors to capture global and local features. Specifically, we represent the global scene as an occupancy voxel grid, while for keyframe-centered local context, we compute Basis Point Set (BPS) scene features using fixed markers on the SMPL body of key poses. Our approach leverages a conditional diffusion model that incorporates keyframes, global scene features, and keyframe-centric local context as conditioning inputs, enabling the generation of high-quality transitions that respect both spatial keyframe and environmental constraints. Experiments demonstrate that our model outperforms baseline methods on the TRUMANS dataset and exhibits promising generalization on unseen phone-scanned scenes from the GIMO dataset. Furthermore, we showcase SceneMI’s capability to handle imperfect scenes and noisy keyframe poses reconstructed from monocular videos, highlighting its potential in broader video-based applications.
