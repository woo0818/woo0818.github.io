SceneMI: Motion In-betweening for Modeling Human-Scene Interactions

Anonymous ICCV submission 4215

Abstract

Acquiring the motions of everyday life is highly challenging due to severe occlusion prominent in typical indoor scenes. However, the surrounding environment can also provide useful evidence of the motion, as the characters in the scene must continuously adjust their positions and posture to navigate or interact with their surroundings. We formulate the complicated problem as motion in-betweening, namely Scene-aware Motion In-betweening (SceneMI), where we complete transitions between noisy keyframes. SceneMI introduces dual scene descriptors composed of global and local features and incorporates comprehensive scene context into the in-betweening framework. Given the sparse estimated keyframe poses and scene reconstruction, the diffusion framework incorporates imputation with scene context to find an arbitrary length of transition with a plausible sequence of actions. The framework of a diffusion process, along with encoded scene context, gains robustness to noisy keyframes or scene models, which is crucial in real-world scenarios. Experimental results demonstrate that SceneMI can significantly improve physical plausibility in reconstructing human-scene interaction from monocular videos. While SceneMI is trained on the high-quality TRUMANS human-scene interaction dataset, it generalizes effectively to the more challenging real-world GIMO dataset, where keyframes are noisy and scenes are captured from phone scans.

Robustness on Noisy Keyframes

Keyframes - Dense, Noisy.

Scene - Hand-Crafted

We provide video comparisons with baselines for scene-aware motion in-betweening under noisy keyframe conditions, using a synthetic noisy TRUMANS test set. Here, we visualize performance in dense, noisy keyframe settings (interval of 3), an extreme case for motion in-betweening that requires handling significant noise. Unlike other motion in-betweening works that require clean keyframes, SceneMI demonstrate robustness to keyframe quality by effectively handling noise during the diffusion process.

Generalization to Real-World

Keyframes - Noisy (Real-World Noise from IMU sensor)

Scene - Phone-Scanned.

We demonstrate the robust generalization of SceneMI using the real-world GIMO dataset. The real-world data GIMO's motion sequences are affected by IMU sensor inaccuracies, and its phone-scanned scenes are significantly differ from training set. By recognizing human-scene interactions from real-world data, we synthesize higher-quality motions that surpass the raw sequences while faithfully preserving the original semantics of the interactions. Here, we uniformly select keyframes at intervals of 30 or 60 frames, corresponding to one keyframe every 1 or 2 seconds.

Video-based Human-Scene Interaction (HSI) Reconstruction

Keyframes - Noisy (Real-World Noise from RGB estimation)

Scene - Video-based Reconstruction.

We highlight the potential of SceneMI by introducing a complete video-based HSI reconstruction pipeline. Here, we demonstrate the results of human-scene interactions from monocular video inputs and SceneMI's crucial role in this pipeline.

Initially, the scenes and motions obtained from our pipeline often exhibit penetration artifacts or jittering due to being obtained independently and affected by occlusion. By applying SceneMI with reconstructed scene geometry and obtained keyframes, we enhance physical plausibility, synthesize natural motions, and complete the video-based HSI reconstruction pipeline.
